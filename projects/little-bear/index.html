How to Make Friends (and Keep Them)

Designing a Storytelling Experience for Children

This project explored how to design a simple and emotionally engaging story for children aged 4–7. Developed as part of a 3D animation course, our team (WEE Studio) created a short narrative about a little bear trying to make and keep a friend.

A story about a curious little bear who wants to make a friend, but discovers that the hardest part is, in fact, keeping him.

The main challenge was to balance emotional expression with visual simplicity, ensuring that the story remained clear, readable, and engaging for a young audience.

Audience

Children aged 4–7; Require clear visual language, expressive elements, and low cognitive load.

Process

The project followed a structured design process, including brainstorming, visual exploration, concept development, storyboarding, and iterative refinement of characters and environments.

Role Specialization

I contributed to the background design, the snowman development, and participated in the early concept research and visual exploration.

Key Design Decisions

A key challenge was designing an environment that felt open and calm, while still maintaining enough contrast for readability.

The snowman was designed not just as a visual element, but as a meaningful part of the narrative, supporting the emotional progression of the story.

Outcome

The final result was a short animated story combining character design, environmental design, and storytelling into a cohesive experience.

Reflection

This project highlighted the importance of simplicity in design, collaboration within a team, and adapting ideas to technical and time constraints.
