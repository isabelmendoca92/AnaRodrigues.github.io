Designing an Emotional Storytelling Experience for Children

A collaborative 3D animation project exploring how visual simplicity, character design, and environment design can support emotional engagement for children aged 4–7.

Overview

This project was developed as part of a 3D animation course, where our team created a short story about a little bear trying to make and keep a friend. The goal was to design a simple and emotionally engaging experience for a young audience.

The Problem

How can we design a story that is emotionally engaging while remaining simple and easy to understand for children aged 4–7?

Audience

The project was designed for children aged 4–7, requiring clear visuals, expressive characters, and a calm and readable environment.

Process

We followed a structured design process including brainstorming, visual exploration, concept development, storyboarding, and iterative refinement of characters and environments.

My Role

I contributed to the background design, the snowman development, and participated in the early concept research and visual exploration.

Key Design Decisions

One of the main challenges was balancing simplicity and expressiveness. The snowy environment needed to feel open and calm, while still providing enough contrast for readability.

The snowman was designed not just as a visual element, but as a meaningful part of the narrative, supporting the emotional progression of the story.

Outcome

The final result was a short animated story combining character design, environmental design, and storytelling into a cohesive experience.

What I Learned

This project reinforced the importance of simplicity in design, collaboration within a team, and adapting initial ideas to technical and time constraints.
